Lisbon
Portugal's capital has it all: museums, parks, churches, restaurants, cafes and more. Located by the sea, the River Tagus and the Port of Lisbon in the south of Portugal, the city of Lisbon is just as historic as Rome and easily one of the most fascinating cities in Europe. See the famous Jeronimos Monastery, Belem Tower, and take in spectacular views of the city, sea and port from the Castelo de San Jorge. You can visit all these sights and more on Lisbon's famous trams, or explore Lisbon's hilly streets on foot. If you're tired after all that sightseeing, relax with coffee and a cake in a cafe - or a much more well-known local delicacy such as Pasteis de Nata - a delicate egg tart made famous by the nuns of Belem.

SJ_B1_076
Azores
It's worth braving what is perhaps one of the most dangerous airports in the world to reach by far the most dramatic scenery in Portugal: The Azores Islands. Much windier than Lisbon, these volcanic islands are almost as close to the US as they are to Europe. Dramatic green scenery and pristine beaches will delight any nature-lover and if you like ocean life, take a boat trip - the Azores are famous for whales and dolphins! The Azores are undoubtedly some of the most beautiful islands in Europe.

Faro
If you want a trip combining city life with surely some of the most extraordinary nature and beaches in Southern Europe, why not head to Faro? Much smaller than Lisbon and with far fewer people, Faro has a historic center with a medieval cathedral and city gates - as well as beautiful beaches and even flamingoes! Known as the sunniest city in Europe, Faro has easily one of the best seaside locations in the area and is considerably more famous for its seafood than many other European cities.

I'm writing this week's entry from Suvarnabhumi Airport while I eat my final meal in Bangkok! I had such an amazing time.
It's the most exciting city I've ever visited by far! There are so many amazing things to see - the most famous places are the Grand Palace, the Golden Mount, and Wat Pho.
Bangkok has so many people! I was amazed! There's easily much more traffic than in my country, and so many skyscrapers. But the temples are very peaceful, and so extraordinary to look at. Thai architecture is so distinctive and Bangkok has such a signature mix of modernity with the Skytrain, skyscrapers, and shopping malls combined with the traditional houses, temples, architecture, as well as the boats and floating markets on the canals.
I was so tired after all that exploring, but I got my energy back with lots of Thai desserts and snacks - I think mango sticky rice is my favorite by far.
I couldn't believe how hot Bangkok is! It's definitely the hottest place I've visited so far. Thankfully, there's a lot of malls where you can take a break from the heat - and you can also buy some of the most amazing souvenirs! I bought some traditional carved soap and a Thai silk scarf. Now I've got just enough time for a glass of Thai milk tea before I board my flight - I can't wait to see what Chiang Mai has in store for me!
Bangkok

Chiang Mai
As soon as I stepped off the plane, I realized Chiang Mai was much colder than Bangkok. I'm so glad I brought my sweater!
I've been here for 2 days and it's already so different from Bangkok. It's a considerably smaller city and so much quieter. It's also significantly older than Bangkok - Chiang Mai was founded in 1296, has over 100 temples, and the Old City is still surrounded by the old walls and moats. The air is much cleaner than Bangkok - and cleaner than my home city, too!
The mountain views are by far the best I've ever seen - seeing the mountains from any point in the city is one of the most impressive sights of my trip so far. This must be by far the most beautiful city I have ever visited!
There's a famous night market, a little bit better than the shopping in Bangkok. Chiang Mai is famous for silver and I saw the most amazing temple made of silver and even bought some silver jewelry! And there's so much amazing food here, too - some of the food is just the same as in Laos, like sticky rice.
This is the most incredible city I've ever been to, by a long way!
